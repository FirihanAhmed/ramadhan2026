Key Insights
13x
Global growth since 2000
In 2000, only 413M people were online, mostly in the US, Europe and Japan. By 2023, 5.4 billion people are connected. The internet grew faster than any infrastructure in human history.
3% to 96%
Oman's leap
Oman went from 3% internet penetration in 2000 to 96% in 2023, a 32x increase. The entire GCC leapfrogged from single digits to near-universal access in two decades.
2.2B
Still offline
Despite the growth, 2.2 billion people remain offline, mostly in Sub-Saharan Africa and South Asia. India alone has around 670M people without internet access.
10 yrs
The fastest leaps
Saudi went from 2% to 70% in 15 years. China brought 1 billion people online in one generation. Morocco: 1% to 88%. Mobile was the great equalizer. Many countries skipped desktop entirely.
